How We Coach

We don't teach grappling like choreography.

Traditional coaching: watch a technique, repeat it fifty times with a compliant partner, try to remember it when it matters. It works — slowly. The problem is that pressure and fatigue scramble memory. Techniques drilled perfectly in class evaporate the moment someone's actually resisting.

We use the Constraints-Led Approach. Instead of drilling isolated techniques, we structure training so the right movements emerge through problem-solving under real pressure. You develop instinct, not recall. Your game — not a copy of someone else's.

Questions

Everything you want to know before you book.

The questions we actually get asked.

Three things. First, we're entirely no-gi — our curriculum and competition focus are built around grappling without the gi. Second, we use the Constraints-Led Approach: a modern coaching methodology that builds adaptable, instinctive grapplers rather than athletes who can only execute techniques in controlled conditions. Third, our coaches have 125+ competition wins between them — their credentials come from performance, not belt colour.

A lot transfers — positional awareness, submissions, pressure. But no-gi has its own game: different grips, faster scrambles, and positions that don't exist in the gi (like the front headlock and bodylock systems). If you've been training gi, you'll pick things up quickly, but expect your game to evolve in ways that also improve your gi work.

Instead of drilling techniques in isolation and hoping they show up under pressure, we structure training so the right movements emerge through guided problem-solving in live scenarios. It's backed by motor learning research and used by elite coaches in wrestling, rugby, and MMA. The result is skills that hold up in competition — not just in drilling.
