The things people worry about

"I'm not fit enough"

Nobody is fit enough for grappling until they start grappling. Fitness comes from training. Every person on the mat built their conditioning by showing up, not by preparing in advance.

"I'll be in everyone's way"

The Foundations class exists specifically for you. It's built around a 4-week beginner curriculum. You won't be thrown in with experienced grapplers before you're ready.

"I'll get hurt"

We have zero tolerance for ego or deliberate roughness on the mat. Coaches supervise every session. You control the pace, and you can stop any time. Your safety is not negotiable.

Your first session

What actually happens
when you show up.

No surprises. No being thrown in the deep end.

01

Arrive & meet the coach

Get to the gym 10 minutes early. A coach will greet you, show you around, and explain how the session runs. No paperwork wall. No hard sell.

02

Warm up together

The class starts with a group warm-up. Nothing extreme. Movements that prepare your body for grappling, explained clearly for first-timers.

03

Learn through games

Instead of memorising techniques, you'll play structured grappling games designed to build real skills. The coach sets a challenge, you figure it out with a partner. You'll be problem-solving, not copying.

04

Build from there

Games get progressively more open as you gain confidence. By the end of your first class, you'll have moved, solved problems, and started developing instincts. No one will ask you to spar before you're ready.

Before your first class

What to wear.
What to bring.

You don't need to buy anything before you come. We'll advise on gear after your first session once you know you want to continue.

  • Shorts — athletic shorts or board shorts. No zips or pockets that could catch.
  • Rash guard or fitted t-shirt — a rash guard is ideal, but a fitted t-shirt works fine for your first class.
  • No shoes on the mat — bring flip-flops or slides for walking around the gym.
  • Water bottle — stay hydrated. There are no fountains on site.
  • Trim your nails — fingernails and toenails, for your training partner's sake.
The class you'll start in

Foundations

A structured class that cycles through core grappling concepts over four weeks. Each week builds on the last, but you can join at any point in the cycle. You won't be expected to remember anything from a previous class.

Revolving 4-week beginner curriculum

Rather than demonstrating techniques for you to copy, the coach sets up structured games and challenges that teach you to move, react, and solve problems on the mat. You develop real instincts from day one, not a list of moves you can't use under pressure. The class covers fundamental positions, movement, escapes, and submissions — all at a pace that works for you.
